<system name="Breathless" version="SRD v1.0" dice="d4,d6,d8,d10,d12,d20" license="CC-BY-4.0" source="https://farirpgs.itch.io/breathless-srd">

<core_mechanic>
Roll a skill die. 1-2: fail (things get worse). 3-4: succeed at a cost. 5+: clean success.
After rolling, the skill die steps down one size (d12→d10→d8→d6→d4).
When a skill reaches d4 and you roll it, it stays at d4 — but you're desperate.
To restore dice, you must Catch Your Breath.
</core_mechanic>

<dice_ladder>
d4 (desperate) → d6 → d8 → d10 → d12 (fresh).
Dice only step DOWN from use. They only step UP from Catching Your Breath.
</dice_ladder>

<character_creation>
Pick a class/role (defined by the setting).
Assign starting dice to skills as defined by the class — typically 3-4 skills at d8-d10.
Each character also has an item or two with their own dice (works like skills but for gear).
Stress track: typically 3-4 boxes. Mark one when you fail a roll or face harm.
When all stress boxes are filled, you're out of action.
</character_creation>

<skills>
Skills are defined by the setting hack — typically 4-6 per character.
Examples: fight, sneak, study, craft, move, charm, shoot.
Each skill has its own die that degrades independently.
</skills>

<catch_your_breath>
A character can Catch Their Breath as an action (takes a moment of calm, not mid-danger).
Reset ALL skill dice to their starting values.
BUT: the GM introduces a complication, threat, or cost. The world moves while you rest.
This is the central tension of Breathless — resources drain fast, recovery has a price.
</catch_your_breath>

<items>
Items have their own dice (like skills). Using an item rolls that die.
After use, the item die steps down. At d4, the item is nearly spent.
Items can be replenished or replaced during downtime or by Catching Your Breath (setting-dependent).
</items>

<combat>
No initiative. Fiction-first — GM describes the threat, players act.
Attack: roll the relevant skill (fight, shoot, etc.). 5+: you hurt them. 3-4: you trade blows. 1-2: you get hurt.
Harm: mark 1 stress. When stress is full, you're out.
The skill die steps down after the roll regardless of outcome.
</combat>

<gm_guidance>
The game is about resource pressure. Skills degrade, items break, stress accumulates.
Every Catch Your Breath should cost something — time, position, safety.
When a player fails (1-2): introduce a new threat, take away a resource, or make the situation worse.
When they succeed at a cost (3-4): they get what they want but something else goes wrong.
Pacing: push hard, then offer a breather. The rhythm of tension and relief is the game.
</gm_guidance>

</system>
