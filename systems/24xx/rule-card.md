<core_mechanic>
Roll a dice pool based on skill + training. Take the highest die.
1-2: disaster (things get worse). 3-4: setback (partial or costly). 5+: success.
If helped by a useful background or item, upgrade the die by one step.
If hindered by an injury or circumstance, downgrade one die.
</core_mechanic>

<dice_ladder>
d6 (untrained) → d8 (trained) → d10 (expert) → d12 (master).
When rolling, assemble a pool: one die for the skill, upgrade/downgrade as applicable.
If you have no relevant skill at all, roll d6.
Always take the single highest result.
</dice_ladder>

<character_creation>
Pick a concept or background (one sentence: "retired soldier," "ship mechanic," etc.).
Choose or assign 3 skills at d8 each. Everything else is d6.
Optionally start one skill at d10 and one at d6 instead (specialist).
Pick 2 items from the setting's equipment list.
HP/stress: characters can take 3 hits before they're out. Fourth hit = dead or retired.
</character_creation>

<skills>
Skills are freeform — defined by the setting hack, not a fixed list.
Typical examples: fight, sneak, charm, hack, pilot, shoot, medicine, survive.
The GM decides which skill applies. If a player makes a good case for an unusual skill, allow it.
</skills>

<combat>
No initiative order. Fiction-first: the GM describes the situation, players declare actions.
Attack: roll the relevant skill die. 5+ = you hit and deal harm. 3-4 = you hit but take harm too (or something goes wrong). 1-2 = you miss and take harm.
Harm = 1 hit by default. Powerful weapons or circumstances may deal 2.
Armor may absorb 1 hit before breaking.
</combat>

<advancement>
After a session or significant accomplishment, a player may:
- Upgrade one skill by one die step (d6→d8→d10→d12), OR
- Gain a new item or contact, OR
- Clear one injury/condition.
Skills cannot exceed d12.
</advancement>

<gm_guidance>
Play to find out what happens. Don't pre-plan solutions.
When a player describes an action, decide: is this risky? If yes, roll. If no, it just happens.
On a setback (3-4), offer a hard choice or partial success, not just "you fail."
On a disaster (1-2), make things actively worse — new threats, lost resources, ticking clocks.
NPCs don't roll. When an NPC acts against a PC, the PC rolls to resist/avoid.
</gm_guidance>

</system>
