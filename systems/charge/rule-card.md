<system name="Charge RPG" version="SRD v1.0" dice="d6" license="CC-BY-4.0" source="https://farirpgs.itch.io/charge-srd">

<core_mechanic>
Action roll: roll a pool of d6s. Take the highest die.
1-3: bad outcome. 4-5: mixed outcome (succeed with cost/complication). 6: full success. Double 6: critical success.
Pool size = action rating (0-4 dots). 0 dots: roll 2d6 and take the LOWEST.
</core_mechanic>

<character_creation>
Pick a concept (one sentence).
Choose 4 action dots distributed among 12 actions (3 categories × 4 actions each):

Physique: finesse, move, muscle, sneak.
Insight: hunt, study, survey, tinker.
Resolve: bond, command, focus, sway.

Each action starts at 0 dots. Assign 4 dots total (max 2 in any one action at creation).
Momentum: starts at 2.
Stress: 4 boxes (clear).
</character_creation>

<action_roll>
When a character attempts something risky:
1. Pick the relevant action (e.g. muscle, sneak, sway).
2. Determine position: controlled (low risk), risky (default), desperate (dire).
3. Determine effect: limited, standard (default), great.
4. Roll action rating in d6s. Take highest.

Results by position:
  Controlled — 1-3: you hesitate (tick clock or lose opportunity). 4-5: you do it but with minor cost. 6: you do it clean.
  Risky — 1-3: things go wrong (harm, complication, lost opportunity). 4-5: you do it but with cost. 6: you do it clean.
  Desperate — 1-3: the worst happens. 4-5: you do it but with serious cost. 6: you do it clean.
  Critical (any position) — you do it with increased effect or bonus.
</action_roll>

<momentum>
Momentum is a shared resource (pool starts at 2, max 6).
Gain momentum: when you succeed with style, when fiction warrants it.
Spend momentum: push yourself (+1d6 to a roll, costs 1 momentum) or assist an ally (+1d6, costs 1 momentum).
When momentum hits 0, the GM can make a hard move or complication.
</momentum>

<stress_and_conditions>
When you take harm or are affected by a complication, mark stress (1-3 boxes depending on severity).
Stress boxes: 4 total. When all 4 are filled, you're taken out.
Conditions: narrative tags applied by the fiction (e.g. "broken arm," "terrified"). The GM uses these to inform position and effect.
Stress clears during downtime or when the fiction allows recovery.
</stress_and_conditions>

<clocks>
Clocks track progress toward goals or escalating threats.
Sizes: 4, 6, or 8 segments.
Tick clocks when the fiction demands it — on action roll results, with time passing, when threats escalate.
When a clock fills, the thing it represents happens (good or bad).
</clocks>

<combat>
No initiative system. Fiction-first: GM describes danger, players declare actions.
Each significant action is an action roll with position + effect.
Harm = stress. Armor may absorb 1 stress before it's used up.
NPC threats are represented by clocks (e.g. "Goblin Pack" 4-clock: fill it to defeat them).
</combat>

<advancement>
After a session or arc, gain 1 action dot (max 4 in any action).
Every 3 sessions (or after a major arc), gain a talent — a special ability that bends one rule.
</advancement>

<gm_guidance>
Default position is risky, default effect is standard. Adjust based on fiction.
Use clocks to pace everything — faction plans, countdowns, long-term projects.
When the player rolls 1-3: follow through on the position's consequences. Don't soften it.
Momentum at 0 is your license to escalate.
</gm_guidance>

</system>
