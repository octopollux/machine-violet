<system name="FATE Accelerated" version="FAE" dice="dF" license="CC-BY-3.0" source="https://fate-srd.com/fate-accelerated">

<core_mechanic>
Roll 4 Fudge dice (4dF: each die shows +1, 0, or -1). Sum them (range: -4 to +4).
Add the relevant approach bonus. Compare to opposition (passive difficulty or active roll).
Fail: total < opposition. Tie: total = opposition. Succeed: total > opposition.
Succeed with Style: total beats opposition by 3+.
</core_mechanic>

<approaches>
Six approaches replace skills. Each rated +0 to +3.
Starting array: one at +3, two at +2, two at +1, one at +0.

Careful: precise, methodical, attentive to detail.
Clever: thinking fast, solving problems, inventing solutions.
Flashy: dramatic, attention-grabbing, stylish.
Forceful: brute strength, direct confrontation, intimidation.
Quick: speed, reflexes, acting before thinking.
Sneaky: stealth, misdirection, subterfuge.

The approach you choose describes HOW you act, not WHAT you do.
</approaches>

<four_actions>
<overcome>
Get past an obstacle. Roll approach vs. difficulty.
Fail: you don't or you do at serious cost (GM decides). Tie: you get a minor version or at minor cost.
Succeed: you accomplish it. Style: you accomplish it and get a boost.
</overcome>

<create_advantage>
Create or discover a situation aspect (a temporary narrative tag with free invocations).
Fail (new aspect): you don't create it, or the enemy gets a free invocation on it.
Fail (existing aspect): the enemy gets a free invocation instead.
Tie: you get a boost (fragile, one-use advantage). Succeed: create/discover the aspect with 1 free invocation.
Style: 2 free invocations.
</create_advantage>

<attack>
Try to hurt someone (physical or mental).
Fail: no effect. Tie: no damage but you get a boost. Succeed: deal shifts of stress = (your total - their defense).
Style: as succeed, or you can reduce damage by 1 to gain a boost.
</attack>

<defend>
Oppose an attack or advantage attempt against you. Reactive — not your turn.
Fail: you suffer the consequences. Tie: look at attacker's action for tie result.
Succeed: you avoid the attack/advantage. Style: you avoid it and get a boost.
</defend>
</four_actions>

<aspects>
Aspects are true narrative facts that can be invoked or compelled.
Types: high concept (who you are), trouble (recurring problem), other character aspects (2-3 more).
Situation aspects: created by create-advantage or environmental facts.
Boosts: fragile aspects with 1 free invocation, then they vanish.
</aspects>

<fate_points>
Start each session with 3 fate points (refresh rate = 3, adjusted by stunts).
Spend 1 fate point to: invoke an aspect (+2 to roll or reroll), declare a story detail, activate certain stunts.
Earn fate points when: an aspect is compelled against you (accept the complication).
Compel: GM offers a fate point to make your life harder via one of your aspects. Accept = take the fate point. Refuse = pay a fate point.
</fate_points>

<stunts>
Special abilities that break a rule. Each character gets up to 3 (more = lower refresh).
Format: "Because I [something], I get +2 when I [approach] to [action] when [situation]."
Example: "Because I am a Cat Burglar, I get +2 when I Sneakily Overcome obstacles related to locks and security."
</stunts>

<stress_and_consequences>
Stress boxes: 3 (marked off to absorb damage, cleared at end of scene).
Consequences: mild (-2, clears after one scene), moderate (-4, clears after one session), severe (-6, clears after one arc).
When hit: absorb shifts with stress boxes and/or consequences. If you can't absorb all shifts, you're taken out.
Conceding before being taken out: you lose, but you get to decide how, and you earn a fate point.
</stress_and_consequences>

<advancement>
Minor milestone (end of session): switch two approach ratings, rename an aspect, change a stunt.
Significant milestone (end of arc): +1 to an approach (max +4), gain a new stunt.
Major milestone (end of campaign arc): +1 refresh, rename high concept if warranted.
</advancement>

<gm_guidance>
Aspects are always true. If someone has "On Fire," they're on fire — act accordingly.
Set passive opposition at +0 (easy) to +6 (legendary). Most obstacles: +2 to +4.
NPCs: nameless (1-2 stress, no consequences), named (full stress + consequences).
Use compels generously — they drive the story and keep fate points flowing.
When in doubt about difficulty: it's +2 (Fair).
</gm_guidance>

</system>
